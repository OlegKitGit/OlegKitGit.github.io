The first attempts to understand and appreciate the art of insane began in XIX century when Parisian doctor Auguste Ambroise Tardieu published a “Medico-Legal Studies on Insanity” in which he reproduced a schizophrenic drawing and indicated psychiatric and artistic interest in such work. In the XX century Marcel Rezh proposed one of the first clinical typologies of the mentally ill. Then there were books of Hans Prinzhorn and John McGregor. In the XXI century, with the advent of new technological solutions, objective methods for studying paintings drawn by the mentally ill appeared. We are currently developing a deep neural network for classifying children's pictures for the purpose of early detection of the risk of mental disorders in children.

To train a neural network, we need a large number of high-quality paintings drawn by the mentally ill. Please send them by email indicating age and diagnosis: thepsychemirror@gmail.com
